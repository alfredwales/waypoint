# Testing Waypoint

Thanks for helping test Waypoint! This is a personal finance app that runs entirely on your own Mac — there's no server, no account, and no data ever leaves your machine. Everything is stored locally in the app.

## Installing

1. Download the latest `Waypoint-arm64.dmg` from the releases page (link provided separately).
2. Open the DMG and drag **Waypoint** into your **Applications** folder.
3. Open Waypoint from Applications. **The first time you open it**, macOS will likely show a warning that it's "from an unidentified developer" (the app isn't signed with a paid Apple Developer certificate). To get past this:
   - **Right-click (or Control-click) the Waypoint icon** in Applications and choose **Open**, then confirm **Open** in the dialog that appears — this only needs doing once.
   - If that doesn't work: go to **System Settings → Privacy & Security**, scroll down, and you should see a message about Waypoint being blocked — click **Open Anyway**.
4. That's it — Waypoint runs fully offline from here. The very first launch will walk you through a short setup (name, currency, your first account).

## Getting updates

Waypoint checks for updates automatically shortly after it opens. If one's available, it'll ask to download it, then open a familiar drag-to-Applications installer window (the same one you used the first time) — just repeat step 3 above (drag to Applications, replace the existing app) and relaunch.

## Reporting bugs

If something breaks or looks wrong, use the **bug icon in the bottom-right corner of the app** — click it, describe what happened, and send it. It'll include some basic diagnostic info (app version, which tab you were on) to help track down the issue, but never any of your financial data.

If the in-app reporter isn't working for some reason, email **alfie@aberrationmedia.co.uk** directly with what you were doing and what went wrong.

## What to test

There's no fixed script — just use it the way you'd actually use a budgeting app: add your accounts, log some income/expenses, try importing a bank statement (PDF or CSV), set up a few budgets, and poke around the Debt, Savings, and Growth tabs if they're relevant to you. Anything that feels confusing, wrong, or broken is worth flagging, even if it's minor.
